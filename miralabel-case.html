Case Coding,

Most facilities don't have a printing problem, they have an identification problem. Cases move too fast, labels fall off, and manual coding creates mistakes that follow products all the way to the customer.

The Problem

Manual case coding
creates downstream problems.

Labels Fall Off, Data Gets Lost

A label gets damaged, falls off, or becomes unreadable. Now no one knows where the product came from, where it is going, or what is inside.

Manual Labeling Slows Everything Down

Operators stop the line to apply labels, reprint bad labels, or fix coding errors. The faster the line moves, the more likely mistakes become.

Wrong Codes Create Wrong Shipments

A missed barcode, incorrect date code, or unreadable print can lead to misrouted products, rejected shipments, or expensive chargebacks.

Coding Problems Start Upstream

A small print issue early in production becomes a bigger problem later. Warehousing, sortation, shipping, and customers all pay the price.

Too Many Label Types to Manage

Different customers, destinations, SKUs, and compliance requirements create constant label changes. Manual processes make every change harder.

Rework Costs More Than Prevention

When a case is coded incorrectly, someone has to stop, pull it, relabel it, and send it back through the line. That adds labor, delays, and waste.
