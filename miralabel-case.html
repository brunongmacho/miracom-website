Case Coding
It Hits the Mark Every Time

Every case needs a clean, readable code. Faded prints and missed codes lead to rework, rejections, and chargebacks. Get it right at production speed.

The Problem

Bad codes slow down
the entire supply chain.

Unreadable Codes Cause Rejection

Smudged, faded, or poorly printed codes get rejected at sortation, at the dock, or worse — at the customer's receiving dock.

Speed Can't Keep Up

Older coding systems cannot keep up with modern line speeds. As production ramps up, codes get skipped or misaligned.

Incorrect Codes Create Wrong Shipments

A single incorrect date code or batch number can flag an entire shipment for rejection — costing you the product and the customer.

Compliance Fails Result in Fines

Missing or incorrect lot codes, expiration dates, or country of origin markings trigger compliance violations and fines.

No Verification Means No Quality Control

If nobody checks whether a code printed correctly, bad codes keep moving downstream until someone finds them — usually the customer finds them.

Rework Burns Labor and Time

When a case leaves your facility with a bad code, someone has to pull it, relabel it, and re-enter it into the system. That is double handling.
