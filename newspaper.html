When Newspaper Production Breaks Down,
Revenue Disappears.

Every missed insert. Every incorrect zoning. Every manual scheduling error. That's money walking out your door.

Operational Pain Points

Your production floor is bleeding money. Here's where:

Constant Pressure to Meet Deadlines

Tighter deadlines. Smaller crews. Increasing variability in insert packages. You're fighting to get every publication out on time — and one mistake means missed delivery.

Growing Complexity in Insert Planning

Multiple product lines. Multiple zones. Multiple versions. Managing it all manually is a mathematical nightmare waiting to fail.

Manual Tracking Errors

Mis-shipments. Wrong inserts in wrong bundles. Customer complaints. Every manual step is an opportunity for error to slip through.

Limited Visibility Across Zones

You can't see what's happening across zones and products. Problems stay hidden until a customer calls — or a truck leaves without what it needs.

Rising Labor Costs

More manual work. More labor hours. More overtime. Your production costs keep climbing while efficiency keeps dropping.

Missed or Incorrect Inserts

Customers receive wrong products. Returns flood in. Brand reputation takes a hit. Every insert error costs more than just the material.

Re-run Costs

When errors aren't caught in real-time, you rerun entire production runs. Multiply that by labor, material, and downtime.

Lack of Real-Time Production Counts

You don't know what's actually been produced until hours later. By then, the damage is done and customers are calling.

Labeling Material Waste

Pre-printed labels sit in inventory, become obsolete, and get thrown away. Every version change means more waste.

Disconnected Systems

Your inserter doesn't talk to your scheduler. Your press doesn't talk to your inventory. Data flows break down at every handoff.

Legacy Hardware Limitations

Old control systems can't handle modern production speeds. They're holding your operation back from what it's capable of.
